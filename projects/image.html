Research Paper

Image to Image Translation Using GAN and U Net for Satellite to Map Conversion

Jan 2024 - Feb 2024

Summary

  • Objective: Developed a GAN-based system to convert satellite images into map format by learning a mapping between source (satellite images) and target (Google Maps) domains.
  • Enhanced Edge Detection: Integrated U-Net for edge detection to improve the GAN's performance in identifying and translating distinct features from satellite images to maps.
  • Dataset: Utilized the pix2pix dataset, which includes 1,096 satellite images of New York and their corresponding Google Maps images. Supplemented with additional images using Google Maps Static API.
  • Methodology: Applied U-Net for segmentation to create well-defined edges, which were then used as input for the GAN. This approach aimed to enhance the clarity of features in the translated images.
  • Preliminary Results: Demonstrated improved edge detection and feature differentiation in translated images compared to traditional GAN methods. Results showed clearer representation of streets and landscapes.
  • Evaluation Metrics: Evaluated the system using Intersection over Union (IoU), Dice Score (F Score), and Pixel Accuracy to assess the accuracy and effectiveness of the image translation.
  • Future Work: Highlighted the potential for refining the approach and applying it to other image translation tasks, based on the initial promising results and insights from existing research.
